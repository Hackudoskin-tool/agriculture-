The Green Revolution

The Green Revolution in the 1960s was a turning point for India. By introducing high-yielding varieties (HYV), advanced irrigation, and modern fertilizers, India transformed from a food-deficient nation to a global agricultural powerhouse.

Ancient Roots

Farming in India dates back to the Indus Valley Civilization. From the domestication of wild grains like wheat and barley in the Neolithic Revolution to modern organic practices, our history is written in the soil.
